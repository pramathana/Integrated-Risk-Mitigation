🎯 Conclusion

Key Points
Risk Profile & Threat Identification
The risk profile was successfully compiled into a Priority Risk List, identifying 11 contemporary threats based on qualitative studies and structured interviews that were verified by internal employees in accordance with DetereCo's operational conditions. There are 3 threats with a Moderate risk level that are the highest priority (T.10, T.11, T.09), followed by 3 threats with Low to Moderate risk (T.04, T.07, T.02), and finally 5 threats classified as Low risk (T.01, T.03, T.05, T.06, T.08).
Universal Risk Modification under ZTA
Although there are 8 threats whose risk levels align with the organization's risk appetite, because the Zero Trust Architecture principle assumes that every threat will be exploited by an attacker and existing control mechanisms have not been implemented in accordance with the 7 Tenets of NIST SP 800-207, the entire Priority Risk List, containing 11 threat risk, falls under the "Risk Modification" response option. This is done to design a Risk Mitigation Program (RMP) based on the principles of Zero Trust Architecture.
Governance & Oversight Deficiencies
The results of the capability level assessment highlight deficiencies in the domain of executive strategic oversight (EDM03.01, EDM03.02, and EDM03.03) stemming from the lack of training programs and the absence of active, substantive oversight by stakeholders. Weaknesses in managerial operational execution in the field (APO12.01, APO12.02, APO12.03, APO12.04, and APO12.06), which collectively constitute the root cause of the company's exposed technical vulnerabilities.
ISO 27005 & COBIT 2019 Integration
The Integrated Risk Mitigation Program was successfully designed by adopting the ISO/IEC 27005 process flow (from context establishment and risk assessment to risk treatment). The 11 priority risks were then precisely mapped to governance practice weaknesses in the COBIT 2019 domains EDM03 and APO12. This mapping demonstrates that the root cause of each technical vulnerability can be directly traced to specific deficiencies in governance management practices that are not yet optimal.
ZTA Tenets & ISO 27002 Translation
Risk treatment recommendations were successfully designed by mapping the root causes of governance issues to the 7 fundamental principles (Tenets) of Zero Trust Architecture. To make them practical, these ZTA principles were translated into technical security controls from ISO/IEC 27002:2022 (such as control 8.12 Data Leakage Prevention, 8.22 Segregation of Networks, 8.5 Secure Authentication, etc.). These recommendations are classified using the 5-Layer Zero Trust Defense Model to mitigate modern threats in a structured manner.
Empirical Validation & Consensus
The Final Integrated Risk Mitigation Program has been enhanced through empirical validation by 9 industry experts and academics, proving the relevance of the artifacts to the final results: 7 out of 11 threats were rated "Highly Relevant" and 4 out of 11 threats were rated "Relevant," and a unanimous consensus (100%) was reached during the Participant Validation phase. This can serve as a blueprint for cybersecurity that precisely aligns strategic risk management gaps with the execution of tactical security controls based on the Zero Trust Architecture principle in the aerospace industry.

💡 Recommendation

Strategic Next Steps
Dynamic HR-IT Integration
Integrate the reporting system for changes in employee status (transfers, retirement, resignation) from the Human Capital (HC) division with the Information Technology (IT) management system. This is to automate the revocation of system access rights in real time (dynamic policy) in order to eliminate reliance on slow and high-risk manual bureaucratic processes.
Institutional MFA Transition
Simulate a phased transition to the use of MFA at the institutional level; if well-received, this can become a mandatory policy issued through a formal instrument (SKEP or Decree).
Mandatory Root Cause Analysis
Formalize the root cause analysis process as a mandatory post-incident procedure by ensuring that the board of directors and senior management are actively involved in periodic risk oversight. Currently, APO12.06 Level 5 is rated zero because root causes are not escalated to the highest decision-makers.
Stricter Intranet Governance
Establish a formal policy regarding stricter restrictions on the use of the Intranet. This aims to protect the company's IP reputation from damage.
Cybersecurity Workforce Training
Invest in cybersecurity workforce competency training through tiered training programs and specialized IT risk management certifications. This is crucial to reduce the risk of future information leaks, as internal employees will have a high level of awareness regarding implicit trust.
DLP & DRM Procurement Priority
The procurement of DLP (Data Leak Prevention) and DRM (Digital Rights Management) systems should be treated as a budget priority that cannot be postponed, as they are one way to replace the bureaucracy of physical paper forms.

A Zero Trust Security Architecture for IoT and Cloud: Conceptualizing a Risk Mitigation Program for Digital Twins in the Aerospace Industry

Research Focus This paper focuses on adapting the Zero Trust framework for machine-to-machine (M2M) interactions to secure the data flow between physical IoT Edge Gateways and Cloud Digital Twins against False Data Injection (FDI) attacks.
Relation to Bachelor's Thesis This paper serves as a technical extension of the framework developed in the Bachelor's Thesis.
